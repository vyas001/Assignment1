Welcome to Wayanad

Wayanad is a rural district in Kerala state, southwest India. In the east, the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary is a lush, forested region with areas of high altitude, home to animals including Asiatic elephants, tigers, leopards and egrets. In the Ambukuthi Hills to the south, Edakkal Caves contain ancient petroglyphs, some dating back to the Neolithic age.

Wayanad is also known for its tea and coffee plantations, which offer scenic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Visitors can enjoy trekking, hiking, and wildlife safaris in the district's many nature reserves and parks.

Top Tourist Spots in Wayanad

Pookode Lake

Pookode Lake is a scenic freshwater lake located in the Wayanad district. It is surrounded by lush green forests and offers boating and other recreational activities to visitors.

Soochipara Falls

Soochipara Falls, also known as Sentinel Rock Waterfalls, is a three-tiered waterfall located in the Vellarimala mountain range. It is a popular destination for trekking and hiking.

Banasura Sagar Dam

Banasura Sagar Dam is the largest earth dam in India and the second largest in Asia. It is located in the Wayanad district and offers beautiful views of the surrounding hills and forests.
